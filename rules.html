All American Catfish Tournaments™
Tournament rules

Safety First:

  • No use of alcohol or intoxicating drugs during the tournament.
  • Weather delays can be made day of the tournament – ideally teams will be informed of rescheduling of events prior to tournament day.
  • Assistance from others – Help other boaters that are in trouble!
  • It is every person’s responsibility to stop, or report, unsafe acts taking place.
  • Contact the Tournament Director regarding all unsafe acts Tim Berger – 913-486-4462

Sportsmanlike conduct:

Although this is self-exlanatory, it is important to cover a few things.

Everyone is expected to treat others professionally during the fishing event, that event timeframe covers from the time you arrive until the time you leave the vicinity of the event. At no time is it acceptable to have a physical confrontation, or to have arguments where other people feel the need to separate individuals.

At no time will any tournament participant get into another person’s property, boat or vehicle without the owner’s permission.

Vulgar or inappropriate acts, or attire, will not be allowed during the tournament. Any conflicts with other individuals outside of the event, needs to remain outside the event.

** Teams being questioned by, or under investigation by the any law enforcement entity for fishing related situations, will not be allowed to enter the tournament until that agency clears them of any wrong doing.

Local, State and Federal law adherence:

Teams must always comply with state fishing and boating regulations, including the use of PFDs and kill switches.

Fishing and boating licenses, and boat insurance are the responsibility of team members.

  • Bait and Baiting – All local laws apply when using bait.
  • No chumming or baiting of fishing holes, or areas, is allowed in preparation of, or during tournaments.

** Teams being questioned by, or under investigation by the any law enforcement entity for fishing related situations, will not be allowed to enter the tournament until that agency clears them of any wrong doing.

Off water hours:

  • For daytime tournaments – teams will need to stay off the water from midnight the night before, until they sign in the morning of the event.
  • For Night tournaments – teams will need to stay off the water from noon the day of the tournament, until they sign in the afternoon of the event.
  • Once you put your boat in the water, you must remain in line of site of the ramp – you cannot take off (out of site) to go get bait and come back.

Sign In/Registration:

  • Cash only the day of event – Pre-registration is available at AllAmericanCatfish.com
  • You must be signed in prior to the captains/safety briefing in order to participate in the initial launch.
  • If you do not make that time, you are still eligible for the late launch once you have been briefed.

Boat Inspections:

  • Boat captains must be willing to allow tournament staff to inspect their entire boat prior to launching.
  • All boats are required to have a livewell, livewells will not be checked as part of registration, until there is a problem.
  • The boat livewell must be large enough to safely support the life of the fish – example a 20 gallon ice chest is not a livewell.

Safety Briefing:

  • Boat captains must be present at the Safety briefing.
  • Briefings will be held approximately 15 to 30 minutes prior to the boat launch intervals start.
  • You must be signed in prior to the captains/safety briefing in order to participate in the initial launch.
  • If you do not make that time, you are still eligible for the late launch.

Launching:

Each launching order will be in 5 boat groups and with approximately 1-minute interval between launches, unless the tournament director says otherwise.
